UI REVISION DIRECTION

We now have an upgraded logo and the entire website UI must be revised around it.

The logo is detailed, heritage-inspired, and uses deep green and warm bronze/gold tones. The website must feel modern, premium, and properly designed in 2026 — not dated, cluttered, cheesy, or like an old-school tradesman website from 2008.

Design goal:
Create a world-class local service website with a strong branded identity, modern spacing, refined depth, elegant borders, soft shadows, excellent responsiveness, and polished animation. The site should feel current, premium, and trustworthy without becoming corporate or flashy.

Visual direction:
- premium local service brand
- modern countryside / outdoors professionalism
- clean, spacious, sharp
- restrained and confident
- grounded, trustworthy, and high quality

Build the UI around these brand cues from the logo:
- primary: deep forest green
- accent: warm bronze / gold
- neutrals: soft off-white, stone, muted warm greys, moss-tinted backgrounds
- dark surfaces: deep green-charcoal, not pure black

UI rules:
- use layered surfaces, not flat white boxes everywhere
- all cards / containers / panels should have proper outlines and tasteful shadows
- use a consistent border radius system; not overly rounded
- use subtle depth and separation between sections
- use elegant hover states and motion
- prioritise premium composition and layout rhythm
- make the gallery, reviews and services feel editorial and branded
- design mobile-first and make the mobile version feel just as premium as desktop

Avoid completely:
- dated tradesman website aesthetics
- bevelled buttons
- harsh gradients
- cheap stock-template layout
- giant centred text blocks with no structure
- overuse of icons in coloured circles
- old-fashioned glossy effects
- default WordPress-looking sections
- “builder template” feel
- anything that looks 2008, 2016 template-ish, or AI-generic

Typography:
- use a modern, premium font system
- body text must be extremely readable
- headings should have character but still feel contemporary
- no cheesy rustic or fake construction fonts
- let the logo carry the heritage feel; the UI should modernise the brand

Section design:
- hero should feel premium and framed, not generic
- service cards should feel substantial and useful
- testimonials should look elegant and trustworthy
- gallery should be highly visual, clean and polished
- CTA sections should feel high-value and well designed, not like ad banners
- footer should feel structured and premium

Motion:
- smooth, restrained, high-end
- reveal on scroll
- subtle hover lift
- image zoom where appropriate
- clean transitions
- no gimmicky animation

Overall standard:
This site should look like a genuinely high-end modern local brand with proper art direction and frontend craft. It must feel intentional, current, and visually strong while still being approachable and grounded.
